Glen & Stacey's Story

Glen and Stacey discovered Pudelpointers over two decades ago and never looked back. What began as a passion for this remarkable breed evolved into a lifelong commitment to producing dogs that excel equally in the field and the home.

They've hunted across the Northern Plains with their dogs—pheasants in South Dakota, ducks in the Missouri breaks, Hungarian partridge in Montana. Every hunt, every season, every dog taught them something new about what makes a truly versatile hunting companion.

In 2010, they founded Dakota Sky on a simple principle: breed for the complete dog. Not just hunting ability. Not just looks. Not just temperament. Every Dakota Sky Pudelpointer must earn its place through proven performance, health, and character.

Today, they maintain a small, carefully selected breeding program on their land in Dickinson. They personally raise every litter, train dogs year-round, board hunters, and remain available to their puppies—and their puppies' puppies—for life.

Our Land

PHOTO: Dakota Sky property — 10 acres of rolling prairie and training grounds, Dickinson ND

Dakota Sky sits on 10 acres just off Highway 10 in Dickinson, North Dakota—the heart of the Northern Plains. This landscape has shaped us and our dogs.

The property includes training grounds with natural upland cover, bird fields for early season work, ponds for water training, and spacious kennels designed for comfort and security.

The rolling prairie, big sky, and honest weather of this region create the perfect training environment for versatile hunting dogs.

This is where our dogs live, train, and develop. This is where families come to meet their future hunting partners. The Northern Plains provide the perfect backdrop for dogs built to handle any condition and any game. We're proud to operate Dakota Sky right here, in the place we call home.

Our Lifetime Commitment

Every dog that leaves Dakota Sky is a reflection of our program. That's why we stand behind them—always.

We provide lifetime breeder support. That means when you buy a Dakota Sky puppy, you're not just getting a dog—you're gaining a partner in that dog's entire life.

Health questions? Training challenges? Behavioral concerns? Life changes require rehoming? We're here. We answer calls. We help problem-solve. We take dogs back if necessary.

This isn't just a tagline. It's how we operate. Glen and Stacey have rehomed dogs, adjusted feeding programs, counseled owners through training plateaus, and maintained relationships with families for decades.
